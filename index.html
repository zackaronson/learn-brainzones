As Featured In:

Teaching your students how to understand their behavior

Marcey Aronson & Debbie Leonard have been helping students and teachers work better together for over 30 years.

After twenty-five years of working with students identified as “at-risk” or “severe behavior handicapped,” we learned the three most important ingredients for “turning bad things into good things, and to let things go” were; mindful awareness, self-esteem, and deposits into the Bank of Success.

We spent the later part of our careers as instructional coaches working with teachers and administrators. Here we learned Adverse Childhood Experiences impact both students and faculty. The human brain is designed to defend and protect, so how do we, as educators, become more responsive and less reactive when we are feeling stressed, overwhelmed, frustrated, or personally affronted?

The answer is with Learn Brainzones. Through a system of Layered Learning, we can teach teachers how to “turn bad things into good things, and to let things go.” In turn, they can share their knowledge and practices with their students.
